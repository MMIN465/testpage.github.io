Abstract

We propose BRiC, a novel test-time adaptation (TTA) framework that enables long-term human motion generation by resolving execution discrepancies between diffusion-based kinematic motion planners and reinforcement learning-based physics controllers.

While diffusion models can generate diverse and expressive motions conditioned on text and scene context, they often produce physically implausible outputs, leading to execution drift during simulation. To address this, BRiC dynamically adapts the physics controller to noisy motion plans at test time while preserving pre-trained skills through a loss function that mitigates catastrophic forgetting.

In addition, BRiC introduces a lightweight test-time guidance mechanism that steers the diffusion model in the signal space without requiring parameter updates. By combining both adaptation strategies, BRiC ensures consistent and physically plausible executions across diverse environments. We validate the effectiveness of BRiC on a variety of long-term tasks, including motion composition, obstacle avoidance, and human-scene interaction, achieving state-of-the-art performance on all benchmarks.

Overall method

BRiC enables robust execution of motion plans generated by a diffusion based kinematic planner through test time adaptation of the physics based controller. (a) The policy is adapted to the target motion plan distribution while avoiding catastrophic forgetting. (b) The policy input state is transformed to account for noise present in physically inconsistent motion plans.
